üåä How the Water Cycle Works:

1. Evaporation: The sun heats water in oceans, rivers, and lakes. The water turns into vapor (gas) and rises into the air.

2. Condensation: Water vapor cools down and forms tiny droplets that make clouds.

3. Precipitation: When clouds get heavy, water falls back to Earth as rain.

4. Collection: Rain flows into rivers, lakes, and oceans, and the cycle starts again!

üåä Natural Water Flow:

Water always flows downward from high places to low places. This is because of gravity pulling the water down. Rain falls on mountains, flows down into streams, then into rivers, and finally reaches the ocean!

üí® About Moving Air (Wind):

Air is all around us! When air moves, we call it WIND. Wind can be gentle like a breeze or strong like a storm.

Wind Rockets: A wind rocket is a light object with a pointed nose and fins. The wind pushes it through the air! The nose cone helps it cut through the air smoothly, and the fins help it fly straight.

How wind helps: Moving air (wind) pushes against objects. We can use wind energy to fly kites, turn windmills, and launch rockets!
